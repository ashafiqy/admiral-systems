## What Changed and Why

### 1. About split into About + People (two pages)
**What:** The "About" section is now two separate pages — one for the firm story/philosophy, and a separate "People" page for team profiles.

**Why you asked for this:** In the meeting, you mentioned wanting team profiles to be clearly visible and separate from the firm overview. This also lets us use a CMS template so when new associates join (you mentioned possibly next year), we just add their info and it automatically appears on the site.

### 2. Practice areas in priority order
**What:** Services listed as Litigation > Arbitration > Criminal Defence > Internal Regulatory Investigations.

**Why:** You confirmed this priority order in the meeting. Litigation gets the most prominent placement as your primary service. Internal Regulatory is last since you mentioned it's not your main focus — we'll add content for it when you're ready.

### 3. No blog at launch
**What:** We're not building a blog or thought leadership section for launch.

**Why:** You said you want to focus on getting a solid, reputable website live first. Content marketing (LinkedIn posts, articles) comes after launch when you're ready. We can always add a blog section later.

### 4. Case Studies page (placeholder)
**What:** The page exists in the sitemap but will have minimal content at launch.

**Why:** Case studies build trust with prospective clients, but writing them takes time and care. Having the page structure ready means we can add them whenever you're comfortable — no rebuild needed.

### 5. FAQ page
**What:** Added a dedicated FAQ page.

**Why:** This is important for SEO and for AI search engines (like Google AI Overviews and ChatGPT search). When people search questions like "how much does a litigation lawyer cost in Singapore?" or "what is minority oppression?", a well-structured FAQ page helps your site appear in those results. We'll draft the questions based on what your potential clients typically ask.

### 6. People page uses a database template (CMS)
**What:** Team member profiles are stored in a database, not hardcoded into the page.

**Why:** You mentioned not needing new associates this year but possibly next year. With the CMS template, adding a new team member is as simple as filling in their name, photo, bio, and role — it automatically appears on the site in the same format as Josh and Wei Ern. No developer needed.
