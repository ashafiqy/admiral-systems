## Executive Summary
*Quick snapshot of the entire project in 2-3 sentences. Read this first to understand what we're building, why it matters, and where we are right now.*

16-week Webflow website redesign for Hop Lun (HK) Ltd to transform their digital presence as a global leader in women's intimates and activewear manufacturing. Project delivers modern, high-performance website with scalable CMS infrastructure supporting commercial enquiries and HR recruitment. Target 50-60% margin with comprehensive design system, Lottie animations, and analytics foundation. Currently in Week 2 (Item Gathering/Wireframe phase) with wireframe review scheduled Week 4.

---

## Strategic Objectives
*What we're achieving for the client beyond just "building a website." These are the business outcomes that matter to Hop Lun's leadership.*

1. **Elevate Brand Perception:** Position Hop Lun as a modern, innovative manufacturing leader differentiating from traditional competitors in the intimate apparel sector
2. **Implement Scalable CMS:** Deploy Webflow infrastructure enabling future multilingual capabilities and autonomous content management by client team
3. **Drive Engagement:** Create compelling user experiences supporting both commercial enquiries (B2B buyers, brand owners) and HR recruitment initiatives
4. **Establish Analytics Foundation:** Implement comprehensive tracking infrastructure (Contentsquare, GA4) for data-driven optimization and conversion measurement

## Scope of Work
*What we're delivering vs. what's not included. Reference this when client asks for something new—is it in scope or a change request?*

### Deliverables Summary
- 8-15 wireframes (responsive: desktop, tablet, mobile)

## Critical Path
*The sequence of milestones that can't slip without delaying launch. If something here is blocked, everything downstream is at risk.*

**Primary Dependency Chain:**
```
Item Gathering (W1) → Wireframes (W2-4) → Visual Design (W5-8) → 
Design Approval (W8) → Dev Start (W9) → Dev Complete (W12) → UAT (W13-14) → Launch (W15)
```

**Bottlenecks:**
- **Wireframe approval gate at W4** - Slip here delays visual design start within same phase
- **Design approval gate at W8** - Must hit this milestone or dev phase compresses dangerously
- **Tiara on critical path for entire design phase (W2-8)** - All visual work depends on her availability (16 pts/week capacity)
- **Client content provision timing** - Currently 2 days delayed on brand assets; impacts wireframe finalization
- **Client review turnaround** - 1-week review windows must be respected or buffer gets consumed
