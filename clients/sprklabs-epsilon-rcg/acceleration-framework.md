# **1\. Executive Summary**

**The situation:** This project has been active since July 2025 with zero revenue generated. The website has slipped three times. Content approval remains the single, consistent blocker. Scott has now flagged urgent content accuracy issues — fabricated statistics, incorrect bio details, and framework rewrites that don’t reflect his actual methodology. We are bearing 100% of costs on a performance-only engagement.

**The root cause:** The team has been creating copy without a verified source-of-truth, leading to invented claims (e.g. “93% success rate,” Scotland Yard references) that Scott never said. This erodes client trust and wastes review cycles. Meanwhile, Scott’s feedback loop is too slow for the pace we need.

**The fix:** Three-pronged approach: (1) a Content Bible compiled from Scott’s own approved documents — no more making things up, (2) an AI-assisted copy workflow where Claude drafts every piece of website copy directly from verified source material, and (3) a clear ownership model across three workstream leads: Daz (Content Lock), Shafiq (Design & Build), Val (Client Review & Go-Live).

# **5\. AI-Assisted Copy Workflow — How We Generate Website Copy at Speed**

This is the workflow that ensures we never again fabricate content. Every piece of copy is generated from the Content Bible using Claude AI, then reviewed by the team before submission to Scott.

## **5.1 The Process (Page-by-Page)**

### **Step 1: Daz Briefs Claude (5 mins per page)**

For each website page, Daz provides Claude with:

* The page name and purpose (e.g. "Homepage hero section", "About Scott page", "Framework overview")

* The relevant source documents from the Content Bible

* The Figma wireframe reference for structure/layout context

* Any specific instructions (e.g. "keep under 150 words", "include CTA to booking page")

### **Step 2: Claude Generates Draft Copy (instant)**

Claude drafts the copy using ONLY facts, phrases, and claims from the 6 source documents. Every paragraph includes a source reference tag so the team can verify provenance. For example:

*Example output: "Scott Walker has spent two decades navigating the world’s most complex negotiations, from kidnap-for-ransom cases to high-stakes boardroom deals. With 300+ cases under his belt and insights featured in the Sunday Times, CNN, BBC, and Forbes, he brings a rare depth of real-world experience to commercial negotiation training."*

*\[Source: Keynote Pitch 2026 — Bio section\]*

### **Step 3: Haykal Reviews for Tone (30 mins per page)**

Haykal checks the draft against the Brand Voice guidelines from the Positioning Statement:

* Professional and authoritative? ✔

* Calm, grounded, supportive? ✔

* Clear, direct, psychologically astute? ✔

* Never aggressive or performative? ✔

* All claims traceable to source documents? ✔

### **Step 4: Daz Compiles into Copy Deck (1 hour total)**

All pages compiled into a single document — structured by page, section, and component. Each section has the copy AND the source reference. This is what goes to Scott.

### **Step 5: Val Sends to Scott (single package, 48hr clock)**

Val sends the complete copy deck as one package (not drip-fed page by page). Clear instructions: "Review and approve within 48 hours per our agreement. Specific change requests welcome; silence \= approval."

### **Step 6: Tiara Pushes to Webflow (upon approval/auto-acceptance)**

Once approved (or after 48 hours), Tiara takes the locked copy and implements in Webflow. No changes after this point without a formal change request logged in Linear.

# **6\. Contractual Leverage Points (SOW Reference)**

**Owner: Val.** These are Val’s tools for keeping the project on track commercially.

## **6.1 Auto-Acceptance Clause**

**SOW Term:** Deliverables submitted for review are deemed accepted if no written feedback is received within 48 hours.

**Application:** Every submission is timestamped in Linear. Val sends a formal email with each submission referencing this clause. If 48 hours pass — it ships.

## **6.2 Client Delay Fee**

**SOW Term:** If the client causes a delay exceeding 7 calendar days beyond a milestone’s agreed date, a monthly delay fee of CHF 800 applies after a 7-day cure period.

**Application:** If Scott’s feedback delays push us past sprint gates, Val formally invokes this clause. The purpose is urgency symmetry — we’re investing time; he needs skin in the game.

## **6.3 Technology Costs**

**SOW Term:** Scott bears all third-party technology costs ($300–$750/month).

**Application:** Val confirms all tool subscriptions are active before Week 4 campaign launch. Any gaps stall activation.
